50 Years of Tattoos

1970s

During this time decolletage tattoos were in style for women. Tattoos were becoming more and more acceptable and women started to be apart of the trend. Small tattoos like Jo Baber's mini heart were popular. This was also an era when Don Ed Hardey tattoos became popular. His style was similar to tattoos of the 1930s. He was known for his traditional style. A popular tattoo of his was a heart with a banner over it.

1980s

As time progressed, tattoos were becoming more main stream and more people were becoming braver with placement. Outerarm tattoos were more often seen. Thick, black line designs like celtic knots were in trend during this time. Mike "Rollo" Malone was a popular artist.

1990s

Lower back and hip tattoos were very trendy, especially for woman. Simple designs like butterflies and stars were often done. Tribal and barbed wire tattoos were popular among men. Sometimes these tattoo trends were blended together to make one tattoo. A popular design would be a tribal butterfly. Guy Aitchison was a popular artist during this time.

2000s

White ink tattoos became a trend in the 2000s. They often did not last but they blended into the skin and usually looked like a beautiful scar, if well done. Scripted tattoos were also popular. People got encouraging words or family names tattooed in a scripted font along their chests, backs, or arms. Jack Rudy was an influential artist during this time.

2010s

Bringing it back to the 70s, small meaningful tattoos became in style again. Some small designs that are popular are the infinty symbol, a semi colon, anchor, and arrows. These design were often placed on fingers or wrists. Now it is becoming trendy to put small tattoos all over your body with no cohesion. As for bigger tattoos, Nikko Hurtado is a popular artist for his realtic art style.
